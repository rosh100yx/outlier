# AI Safety Policies & Governance

Outlier isn't just a tool; it's a movement towards responsible AI adoption in software engineering. We believe that AI should **augment** human mastery, not replace it.

## The Deskilling Threat
When developers rely entirely on LLM agents to generate architecture, they experience "Cognitive Offloading." The neural pathways required to debug, secure, and maintain complex state begin to atrophy.

### How to Contribute to AI Safety
We actively want the community to help us refine our policies! 
1. **Submit Empirical Data:** Run `outlier status` and share your stats. We use aggregate, anonymized data (only when voluntarily provided by users via GitHub Issues) to track industry-wide deskilling trends.
2. **Propose New Guardrails:** Do you have an idea for a pre-commit hook that prevents junior developers from merging unchecked AI code? Open a PR or submit an idea via the `outlier confessional` command.
3. **Draft New Frameworks:** We welcome sociologists, ethicists, and senior engineers to draft new `.yaml` policy profiles that teams can adopt globally.

Join us in ensuring developers remain High-Agency operators, not passive NPCs.
